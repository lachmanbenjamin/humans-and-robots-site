Where the leaks are

You're already good at the work. The money leaks happen everywhere else.

Most local service businesses we talk to have the same handful of problems. None of them are exotic. All of them are fixable. Here's what we usually find on the first call.

  • You're hard to find on Google.Profile half-filled, no recent reviews, competitors above you in the map pack.
  • Your social pages look quiet.Last post months ago. No replies. New customers check, then keep scrolling.
  • Missed calls aren't followed up.Whoever calls back first wins the job. Often, that isn't you.
  • Estimates take too long.The walkthrough was Monday. By Friday they've booked someone else.
  • Customers don't get updates."On the way," "running late," "job's done" — handled inconsistently, if at all.
  • The customer list lives in three places.A notebook, a spreadsheet, and somebody's phone. So nobody can use it.

We help you fix these one at a time, starting with the one costing you the most.

What changed: We pulled 8,000 records out, cleaned them up, and stood up a working CRM with follow-up sequences. Nonprofit · Paid social & community

Active social and ad campaigns for a major nonprofit

Problem: Quiet pages, irregular posting, and no clear sense of what was driving event registrations. What changed: A consistent content calendar across Facebook and Instagram, paid campaigns tied to event registrations, and monthly readouts on what was actually working. Outcome: A social presence the team can keep running, with attribution back to event sign-ups.

Fundraising · Document & pitch

$1M campaign offering, ready in one working session

Problem: A disability-innovation nonprofit had a $1M campaign document going out to 25 family offices — and prospects didn't understand where the money would go. What changed: We rebuilt the offering and added a 1-page ROI leave-behind. Outcome: A board-ready pitch the team could send the next day, with much clearer answers to the obvious donor questions.

What changed: A lean monthly content engine — scripts, shot lists, real-people footage — managed across Instagram and Facebook.
